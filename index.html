About quick story

What I work on
Dynamics ↔ Structure

My main theme is connecting network structure to collective behavior. In my thesis, I study exploration–imitation competition with degree-dependent imitation: higher-degree nodes imitate low-degree nodes less, low-degree nodes imitate high-degree nodes more, and neighbor selection can be biased by degree.

  • ABM simulations + statistical-physics framing (steady states, stability, variance).
  • Network measures: degree distribution, clustering, path length, centralities, Laplacian spectrum.
  • Clear deliverables: reproducible code, LaTeX-ready tables, publication-quality figures.

Research highlights

MSc Thesis — Degree-dependent exploration–imitation dynamics on networks
Isfahan University of Technology (IUT)
2024 — 2026 (edit)

Extends the “exploration–imitation competition” framework by making imitation probability depend on node degree, and by biasing neighbor selection toward high-degree nodes. Exploration is modeled with Gaussian noise for analytical and computational clarity.

  • Findings: steady-state variance persists even with degree-dependent imitation and structured networks.
  • Tooling: large parameter sweeps, uncertainty quantification (SEM), and network-metric ↔ outcome maps.
Centrality-correlation analysis of real vs model networks
Enzyme / biological interaction networks • ER / WS / BA baselines
2025 — Present

Computes correlation heatmaps across multiple centralities (degree, eigenvector, betweenness, closeness, Katz, information centrality, etc.) and uses correlation structure as a “fingerprint” to compare real networks to ensembles of model networks.

  • Observed: Watts–Strogatz often best matches enzyme networks; BA sometimes outperforms.
  • Next step: compare structural features of the best-fit model ensembles against real networks.
