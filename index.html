I recently completed my Master’s in Complex Systems through the lens of statistical physics. My thesis extended exploration–imitation dynamics with degree‑dependent biases, revealing how network structure shapes collective opinion and variance.

My research interests lie at the intersection of network science, stochastic dynamics, and machine learning. I’m particularly drawn to biophysics and neuroscience, econophysics and social physics, and the mathematical foundations of AI, wherever complex, interconnected systems produce emergent, data‑rich behaviour. I’m actively seeking PhD positions where I can apply computational modeling, network science, and machine learning to fundamental or applied problems in these areas.

Research Projects

Impact of Vertex Degree on Exploration–Imitation Dynamics 2024 – 2025
M.Sc. Thesis · Supervisor: Dr. Keivan Aghababaei Samani, IUT
  • Extended a social learning model with degree‑dependent imitation strength and degree‑biased neighbor selection.
  • Simulated four model variants on eight synthetic networks (ER, WS, BA, modular, etc.).
  • Found that degree‑dependent imitation amplifies opinion diversity in heterogeneous networks; degree‑biased selection reduces diversity in modular networks.
  • Power‑law decay of hub influence in BA networks; combined biases yield intermediate steady‑state variances.
  • Poster at 30th Annual IASBS Meeting on Condensed Matter Physics (May 2025); manuscript in preparation.
View code (GitHub) Thesis Abstract & Signature
Entropy‑Based Analysis of Centrality Distributions 2025
Supervisor: Prof. Farhad Shahbazi, IUT
  • Computed Shannon entropy of multiple centrality distributions for real‑world and synthetic networks.
  • Matched with ER, configuration, BA, and WS models.
  • Random models exhibit higher, uniform entropy; real networks show lower, varied entropy.
  • Introduced joint entropy of centrality correlations as a higher‑order complexity measure.
  • Poster accepted at Sharif University of Technology (conference postponed due to unforeseen circumstances).
View code (GitHub)
Modeling Enzyme Network Structure via Centrality Correlations 2025
Supervisor: Prof. Farhad Shahbazi, IUT
  • Used correlation matrices of seven centrality measures to compare 10 enzyme networks with classical generative models.
  • Watts–Strogatz (p=0.01) best reproduced correlation patterns in 9 of 10 cases.
  • Proposed hybrid models for improved reconstruction of biological network structure.
  • Oral presentation at Iran Physics Conference, Yasouj University (Oct 2025).
